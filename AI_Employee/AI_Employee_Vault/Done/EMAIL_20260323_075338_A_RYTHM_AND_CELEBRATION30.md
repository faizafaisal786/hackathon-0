On December 16, 1995—thirty years ago—Dorothy Kangangye Rwetsiba and I were married in Kigali, Rwanda. There was no champagne, no wedding cake, no spectacle. We were revolutionaries, intent on frugality, convinced that meaning lay not in excess but in purpose. Local brew and soft drinks were served. Trust was fragile, truth contested, and the future uncertain. Love itself felt audacious in such a landscape.
